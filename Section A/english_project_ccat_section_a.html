SPOTTING ERRORS (~2 to 3 Qs) Top rules C-CAT tests: • Subject-verb agreement — singular subject takes singular verb • Article misuse — a/an/the confusion • Preposition misuse — at/in/on/for/since questions FIRST, then read the passage • Answers are always directly in the passage — never guess from general knowledge • For tone questions: pick neutral unless the passage is clearly emotional • For title questions: pick the option that covers the whole passage, not just one paragraph 🟡 MEDIUM PRIORITY — know the key rules: SENTENCE COMPLETION (~2 Qs) • Read the full sentence for tone — positive or negative • Eliminate options that break the tone • If a blank follows a contrast word (but, however, although) — the answer contrasts what came before ARTICLES (~1 to 2 Qs) • "a" before consonant sounds, "an" before vowel sounds (sound matters, not spelling) • "the" for specific known things — "I saw the dog" (we both know which dog) • No article for general plural nouns — "Dogs are loyal" not "The dogs are loyal" PREPOSITIONS (~1 to 2 Qs) Key preposition rules: • in (month/year/city) → in July, in 2024, in Mumbai • on (day/date/surface) → on Monday, on 5th July, on the table • at (specific time/place) → at 5pm, at the station • since (point in time) → since 2020 • for (duration) → for 3 years 🟢 LOW PRIORITY — one strategy tip only: IDIOMS AND PHRASES (~1 Q) • Treat as vocabulary — you either know it or you don't • Eliminate options that are too literal — idioms never mean what they literally say • Pick the option that fits the overall sentence meaning ARRANGEMENT OF SENTENCES (~1 Q) • Find the opening sentence first — it introduces a topic, has no pronoun starting it • Find the closing sentence — it concludes or summarises • Build the middle by finding logical cause-effect or time sequence links ════════════════════════════════════════ VOCABULARY BUILDING — fast track method ════════════════════════════════════════ Since I cannot memorise hundreds of words
